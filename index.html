CITIZENS TO ELECT JEFF THURMOND

Experience

"I AM NOT A POLITICIAN, and I won't pretend to be one", says Jeff Thurmund. He is the Senior Franklin Co. Highway Dept. Supervisor of District 2 and has 30 years of experience with the Franklin County Highway Department.

 

Community

I support organizations that strive to make not only Franklin County, but our Country a better place to live and grow up in. I believe that we must teach our youth to protect and learn from the land that provides the very food we depend on to survive. As my children were growing up they became involved in 4-H and FFA programs who’s main goals are to teach these values and life lessons. I am a Franklin County FFA Foundation Board Member, and I serve on the Sullivan FFA Advisory Board. Youth Gun Safety programs, and the protection of our 2nd. Amendment are something I believe in, and are a big part of who I am. Hunting is a way of life for most Franklin County kids, I want to make sure they have programs available to them that will teach them to hunt safely. I strongly support the NRA, and serve proudly on the Meramec Area Friends Of NRA Committee helping to raise grant dollars for Youth Gun Safety programs all across Missouri. I’m a member of the Meramec Area Sportsman’s Association of Sullivan that offers scholarships to graduating FFA and 4-H high school seniors to help further their education. I am also a member of the Fraternal Order Of The Eagles.

 

Family

I was born in St Louis in 1964 but have lived in the Sullivan, Stanton Missouri area my entire life. My parents are Jerry and Sylvia Thurmond of Stanton Mo.
My father owned and operated two successful businesses in Sullivan. He is probably best known for his musical talent and abilities, a little rubbed off on me.
My mother isn’t a native of Sullivan but has lived here since she met and married my father at the age of 17. Most of her life has been spent loving and caring for her family.
I have two younger sisters who also call Stanton Mo their home. I have been blessed with two daughters, a son and 5 perfect in every way grandchildren. I am married to my best friend and the love of my life, Peggy Thurmond. My parents, and Peggy and I live on two sections of what was once my families ancestral home place dating back to the 1800’s  The log cabin that my Great Great Grandfather Isaac Woodcock raised his family in still sit on a section of the same piece property. Some say I not only resemble my Great Great Grandpa Isaac Woodcock, but that I am also trying to follow in his footsteps. Great Great Grandpa Woodcock served as Franklin County Road Overseer from 1881 to 1924.
